# About the Africa Waste Observatory

## Our Vision

To become the leading platform for waste management research and knowledge exchange in Africa, contributing to sustainable development and environmental protection across the continent.

## Our Mission

The Africa Waste Observatory aims to advance waste management practices through:

- Comprehensive research and data collection
- Knowledge dissemination and capacity building
- Policy support and technical guidance
- Stakeholder collaboration and networking

## History

Founded in 2024, the Africa Waste Observatory emerged from a recognition of the critical need for centralized, accessible information on waste management practices across Africa. The observatory was established through collaboration between leading research institutions, environmental organizations, and government agencies.

## Our Approach

We employ a multi-faceted approach to address waste management challenges:

### Research Excellence

We conduct and publish rigorous academic research on all aspects of waste management, from collection and sorting to treatment and disposal.

### Data-Driven Insights

Our comprehensive database tracks waste generation, collection rates, recycling initiatives, and policy implementation across African nations.

### Capacity Building

We develop educational resources and training materials to strengthen waste management capacity at all levels.

### Policy Support

We provide evidence-based recommendations to support policymakers in developing effective waste management strategies.

## Core Values

- **Excellence**: Commitment to high-quality research and analysis
- **Accessibility**: Making information freely available to all stakeholders
- **Collaboration**: Working together across borders and disciplines
- **Sustainability**: Promoting environmentally sound practices
- **Innovation**: Encouraging new approaches and technologies
